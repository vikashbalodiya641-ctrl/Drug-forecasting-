📌 Forecasting Insight: Keytruda's post-2028 trajectory was modeled using weighted erosion benchmarks from two categories of reference drugs. Humira was used as a high-revenue biologic erosion benchmark, while Rituxan and Herceptin were used as oncology-specific analogs — this combination captures both the revenue-scale dynamics and the therapeutic-class erosion behavior most relevant to Keytruda. Humira lost ~78% revenue within 3 years of biosimilar entry. Herceptin lost ~60% within 3 years. Keytruda's base case assumes a ~55% erosion over 5 years (2029–2033) — slightly slower due to (1) combination therapy stickiness, (2) expanded indications across 35+ tumor types, and (3) physician familiarity creating brand loyalty not seen with small-molecule generics like Lipitor. The bull case assumes Merck's label expansions and adjuvant therapy demand slow biosimilar uptake significantly.

Project Methodology

How the forecasting model was constructed — step by step approach

Step 01
Historical Data Collection
Collected Keytruda annual revenue 2014–2028 and calculated YoY growth rate and CAGR (79.7%) to understand the drug's launch trajectory
Step 02
Competitor CAGR Benchmarking
Analyzed 4 reference drugs: Rituxan (17.0%) & Herceptin (15.5%) as oncology-specific biosimilar analogs; Humira (13.7%) as a high-revenue biologic erosion benchmark & Lipitor (5.2%) as a high-revenue small-molecule patent cliff reference
Step 03
Biosimilar Erosion Modeling
Used Humira's biosimilar erosion curve (78% revenue loss in 3 years) and Herceptin's curve (60% loss) as primary and secondary erosion benchmarks post-2028
Step 04
Scenario Construction
Built Bull (5% erosion/yr), Base (10-20%/yr), and Bear (20-35%/yr) cases incorporating market growth rates (6-10% near-term, 7-9% mid-term) and biosimilar share assumptions
Step 05
Direct Competitor Analysis
Compared Keytruda vs Opdivo (CAGR 52.1%) and Libtayo (CAGR 61.8%) — both PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors in the same therapeutic class
Step 06
Biosimilar Landscape
Identified 3 officially announced biosimilar developers (Formycon, Samsung Bioepis, Alvotech+Dr. Reddy's) targeting 2028–2030 market entry aligned with patent cliff
Forecast Assumptions
Forecast scenarios are derived from comparable oncology therapies (Rituxan, Herceptin), high-revenue biologic erosion benchmarks (Humira), market growth projections, and projected biosimilar competition following loss of exclusivity. The 2028 primary patent benchmark reflects the composition-of-matter patent; secondary patents and litigation outcomes may affect the actual exclusivity window.
Data sourced from Merck Annual Reports, BMS Investor Presentations, GlobalData, DrugPatentWatch, and company press releases.
